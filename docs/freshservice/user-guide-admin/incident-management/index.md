<h2>Incident Management</h2><p><br></p><p dir="ltr">Simply put, an incident is an unplanned interruption to an IT service or a reduction in quality of an IT service.</p><p dir="ltr"><br>Incident Management is responsible for proper logging, analyzing and (especially) resolving incidents. It's primary objective is to help resume service operations as quickly as possible.</p><p><br></p><h3 dir="ltr">Incident Management in Freshservice</h3><p><br></p><p dir="ltr">Every time a customer asks for support or submits a query in through the support portal, it becomes a ticket.&nbsp;</p><p dir="ltr"><br>The ticket is now your means of interacting with the customer and every conversation is<br>recorded and can be referred back to, if need be. It should be noted that every customer<br>interaction can become a ticket if required, from email, your website, Social Media, phone calls<br>or any other means.</p><p><br></p><h3 dir="ltr">Creating a Ticket</h3><p><br></p><p dir="ltr">To create a ticket,&nbsp;</p><p><br></p><p dir="ltr">â—Click <strong>New</strong> in the top-right corner.<br>â— Select <strong>Incident</strong> (depending on the request type).</p><p><br></p><p dir="ltr"><img src="https://s3.amazonaws.com/cdn.freshdesk.com/data/helpdesk/attachments/production/50005204005/original/iZirmxuvwGWX1FsmTdeRFuk4UwjMGE1cVg.png?1649416207" style="width: auto;" class="fr-fil fr-dib" data-attachment="[object Object]" data-id="50005204005">â— Enter the details in Submit a ticket form.â— Attach supporting files if necessary<br>â— Click on <strong>Save</strong>, <strong>Save and Close</strong>, or <strong>Save and New</strong> to save changes.</p><p><br></p><h3 dir="ltr">Why the different 'Save' options?</h3><p dir="ltr"><br>While creating a ticket, you can use the most relevant option. Here's what they do:</p><ul><li dir="ltr"><p dir="ltr"><strong>Save</strong> - Save changes and create the ticket.</p></li><li dir="ltr"><p dir="ltr"><strong>Save and New</strong> - Save changes, create this ticket and then create another ticket.</p></li><li dir="ltr"><p dir="ltr"><strong>Save and Close</strong> - Save changes, create the ticket and change its status to 'closed'. Use the<br>option when you need to just document an incident etc.</p></li></ul><p><br></p><p><img src="https://s3.amazonaws.com/cdn.freshdesk.com/data/helpdesk/attachments/production/50005204014/original/dKM79FPq5X5EoSUTOEZ5PzAlxmMHjN3uGw.png?1649416252" style="width: auto;" class="fr-fil fr-dib" data-attachment="[object Object]" data-id="50005204014"></p><h3 dir="ltr">Ticket Acknowledgement</h3><p><br></p><p dir="ltr">Once a customer sends a service query, a complaint, or a request to your company's support, they will automatically receive an email acknowledging that their request has been received.</p><p><br></p><p dir="ltr">There will also be a link to your support portal where the customer can check the status of the Ticket &amp; add comments.</p><p><br></p><h3 dir="ltr">Replying to, and resolving a ticket</h3><p><br></p><p dir="ltr">When a requester raises a ticket, it becomes an 'open' ticket. You can respond to it by clicking on Reply and your answer will be sent to the customer's email as well as get posted on the tickets page itself. It will create a thread, which even the customer will have access to. This gives you a clear view of the complaints received, and the support extended to a specific customer minimizing confusion and repetition of queries from your support staff's side.</p><p dir="ltr">Once a ticket has been replied to, the status can be changed to Pending, and if the customer's problem has been solved, the status can be changed to Resolved. Resolved tickets will be closed after a specific amount of time (that can be set/changed by an admin).</p><h4 dir="ltr"><br></h4><h4 dir="ltr"><strong>Quick Tip:&nbsp;</strong></h4><p><br></p><p dir="ltr">While replying to the requester, you can save a lot of time by including solution articles or canned responses.&nbsp;</p><h3 dir="ltr"><br></h3><h3 dir="ltr">Key Actions</h3><p><br></p><p dir="ltr">Once a ticket is created, you can perform the following actions to it from your Freshservice account.&nbsp;</p><p><br></p><p dir="ltr"><strong>Reply</strong> - You can reply to the ticket, which will be sent as an email to the requester.&nbsp;</p><p dir="ltr"><strong>Forward</strong> - You can forward the entire conversation thread to another agent or person.&nbsp;</p><p dir="ltr"><strong>Add note</strong> - You can add a private note where you can enter key details about a ticket which you want to keep for internal purposes. This will be of great help if the ticket is transferred or assigned to another agent in the future.&nbsp;</p><p dir="ltr"><strong>Discuss</strong> - If you wish to discuss about the ticket with your fellow agents, you can make use of this option. This will open up a chat window where you can add your fellow agents, chat, share relevant files and even highlight key areas in a ticket. You can associate your ticket to a new project</p></li><li dir="ltr"><p dir="ltr"><strong dir="ltr">Project</strong> <strong dir="ltr">Task&nbsp;</strong>- You can associate your ticket to an existing project task</p></li></ul><h3 dir="ltr"><img src="https://s3.amazonaws.com/cdn.freshdesk.com/data/helpdesk/attachments/production/50005204042/original/SdiJqOV1NBRojDa4psqxZnFbPOnPzJfsBQ.png?1649416414" style="width: auto;" class="fr-fil fr-dib" data-attachment="[object Object]" data-id="50005204042"></h3><h3 dir="ltr">More Actions</h3><p><br></p><p dir="ltr">Additional actions include:&nbsp;</p><p><br></p><ul><li dir="ltr"><p dir="ltr"><strong>Merge</strong> -
